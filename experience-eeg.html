At UC Davis Health's Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine, I contributed to cutting-edge research involving electroencephalogram (EEG) technology to study brain activity patterns. My work focused on detecting the state of drowsiness induced by propofol using spatial filters and machine learning algorithms on EEG recordings. I was responsible for data collection, signal preprocessing, and assisting in the development of predictive models that analyze neural responses to anesthesia. This experience strengthened my skills in Python, MATLAB, and biomedical signal processing, while providing valuable insight into the intersection of healthcare and data science.
